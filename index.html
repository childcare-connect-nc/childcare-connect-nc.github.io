Why I built this

From one parent to another

ChildCare Connect started the way most needed things do — with a frustrating search of my own. Like every parent looking for childcare, I had a list of questions I couldn't get answered without booking tour after tour: Is this place reputable? Are they certified? What does it actually cost? Is there a waiting list — and how long? How many kids per teacher? What's their approach? There was no way to know the basics before spending a Saturday somewhere that was never going to be a fit.

I'm Nikieta. I'm the kind of person who wants to validate everything before I trust it — and nothing deserves that more than the people caring for our kids. That's the whole idea behind this site: the essentials about local providers, gathered in one place, so you can compare honestly and reach out only when it feels right. It's also why I personally review every listing before it goes live.

N Nikieta
Founder, ChildCare Connect

FAQ

Good questions

Is ChildCare Connect free for families?

Yes. Searching, browsing profiles, and contacting providers is free for families. Optional premium features may come later, but the core experience stays free.

How are providers verified?

Providers share their licensing and credentials when they join, and we review every listing before it goes live. We also encourage families to verify credentials directly — North Carolina publishes every licensed facility's rating and history, and we're building toward integrated background checks as we grow.

What does "founding provider" mean?

Providers who join the waitlist before launch get their first 6 months listed completely free — no card required. You'll be among the first families see when we go live.

When do you launch?

We're starting in Wilmington, then Raleigh and Charlotte — with Greensboro, High Point, and Durham planned as our next expansion. Waitlist members hear first — that's the whole point of the list.
