4. Parental Due Diligence Requirements

By using ChildCare Connect to find a provider, parents and guardians expressly acknowledge and agree that they bear full and sole responsibility for conducting their own independent due diligence before engaging any provider. This includes, but is not limited to:

4.1 Background & Identity Verification

  • Conducting your own independent background check through a licensed screening service, regardless of any badge displayed on the Platform
  • Verifying the provider's identity with a valid, government-issued photo ID
  • Checking sex offender registries in your state and any state where the provider has lived
  • Searching court records for criminal history

4.2 Licensing & Credential Verification

  • Verifying that any daycare center or in-home care facility holds a current, valid license from the appropriate state or local regulatory authority
  • Confirming CPR and first aid certifications are current and valid
  • Verifying any educational credentials or professional certifications claimed by the provider
  • Checking with the relevant licensing body that no complaints or violations are on record

4.3 Reference Checks

  • Contacting and speaking with a minimum of two (2) professional references provided by the provider
  • Speaking with other families who have previously used the provider's services
  • Asking providers detailed questions about their experience, approach, emergency procedures, and discipline philosophy

4.4 In-Person Evaluation

  • Visiting the childcare facility or home in person before any care begins
  • Observing how the provider interacts with children
  • Inspecting the physical environment for safety hazards
  • Confirming appropriate child-to-caregiver ratios
  • Reviewing emergency procedures and safety protocols

4.5 Legal & Contractual Protections

  • Entering into a written childcare agreement directly with the provider that covers rates, schedule, duties, termination, and emergency contacts
  • Consulting with an attorney if needed regarding your legal rights and protections
  • Understanding North Carolina's laws regarding licensed vs. license-exempt childcare providers
